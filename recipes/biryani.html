Biryani

Description:

Biryani is a fragrant and flavorful South Asian rice dish that beautifully blends basmati rice with tender meat, aromatic spices, and herbs. Whether made with chicken, beef, mutton, or even vegetables, biryani is known for its rich layers of flavor and its enticing aroma. Each bite offers a perfect balance of spice and savoriness, often enhanced with ingredients like saffron, fried onions, yogurt, and fresh coriander. Traditionally cooked using the "dum" method, where the pot is sealed and slow-cooked, biryani is more than just a meal — it’s a celebration on a plate.

Ingredients:

  • Basmati Rice
  • Meat (Chicken, Mutton, Beef, or Fish)
  • Yogurt
  • Onions
  • Garlic and Ginger
  • Tomatoes
  • Herbs (Coriander, Mint)
  • Whole Spices (like Cinnamon Stick, Cardamom Pods)
  • Salt
  • Saffron

Steps:

  1. Mix chicken with yogurt, spices (turmeric, chili, garam masala), ginger-garlic paste, lemon juice, and herbs. Let it rest for at least 1 hour.

  2. Soak basmati rice for 30 mins. Boil with salt and whole spices (bay leaf, cardamom, cloves) until 70% cooked. Drain and set aside.

  3. Fry onions until golden. Set some aside. Cook marinated chicken with the rest of the onions until almost done.

  4. In a pot, layer chicken and rice. Add fried onions, mint, coriander, garam masala, and saffron milk (optional).

  5. Cover tightly and cook on low heat for 20–30 minutes.

  6. Gently mix and serve hot with raita or salad.
